Hello Samruddhi 🌸

A small message from Atharva

When I look back at these years, one thing I know for sure is that meeting you became an important part of my college journey.

Before knowing you, I never really understood why people talked so much about having a special person who could leave a lasting impact on their life. But over time, I realized that some people influence us without even knowing it. In your own way, you became one of those people for me.

Getting to know you taught me a lot about myself. It helped me experience emotions and feelings that I had never experienced before. Before this, many of those emotions were completely unfamiliar to me.

For the first time, I understood what it feels like to genuinely like someone, to think about them unexpectedly, and to appreciate their presence in your life. Those experiences taught me a lot, and for that, I will always be grateful.

I also want to be honest and say that, somewhere along the way, I came to like you. But what matters most to me now is not whether those feelings were returned. What matters is that I had the opportunity to know someone I genuinely admired, respected, and appreciated.

Please don't feel any pressure from anything I have written. You don't owe me an answer, and I am not expecting anything in return. I simply didn't want to leave college carrying words that I never had the courage to say.

One small thing I would like to mention.

If I ever seemed awkward, overly direct, or unsure of how to express myself, I hope you'll forgive me. Many of these emotions and conversations were completely new to me.

To be honest, I spent much more time overthinking whether I should write this letter than actually writing it. But I felt that being honest was better than wondering "what if" years later.

You may remember that I once asked about giving you a gift. The truth is that it was only meant as a small gesture of appreciation. I had thought of giving you lilies because I felt they suited your personality, along with a simple photo frame as a memory. But I completely respect your decision and understand your preference about accepting gifts.

And one small note.

The flower theme wasn't random. I remembered that you like flowers, so I ended up designing the entire website around them.

And before this letter ends, I just want to say that I completely respect whatever you think about it. My intention was never to create pressure or expect anything in return. I simply wanted to be honest and express my appreciation for someone who became a meaningful part of my college journey.

And if, years from now, you ever happen to look back on our college days, I hope you remember me as the person who quietly fell for you, genuinely respected you, and was grateful to have known you.

Thank you for being a memorable part of my college journey.

Take care.

— Atharva
